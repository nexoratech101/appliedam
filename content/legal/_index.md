## Who We Are

AppliedAM (`appliedam.net`) is an independent publication covering additive manufacturing - research, industry applications, and practitioner case studies. We publish submitted work and original editorial content for the AM community.

---

## Information We Collect

We keep this minimal by design.

**When you contact or submit to us**, you send us your name, email address, and whatever content you include in your message. We use that information only to respond to you and, if your submission is accepted, to publish your work.

**When you visit the site**, standard server logs may record your IP address and browser type. We do not use tracking cookies, and we do not run advertising networks or behavioural analytics.

**Google Fonts** - our site loads fonts (Space Grotesk, Inter) from Google's servers. This means your browser makes a request to Google when you visit. We have no control over what Google does with that data; their privacy policy applies.

We do not sell, share, or trade your information with anyone.

## Submissions and Your Content

When you submit work to AppliedAM - whether through /submit/ or /write-for-us/ - here is what you are agreeing to:

- **You own your work.** Submitting to us does not transfer your copyright. You keep full ownership of everything you submit.
- **You are giving us permission to publish it.** By submitting, you grant AppliedAM a non-exclusive, royalty-free licence to display, publish, and promote your work on this site and in connection with AppliedAM's presence.
- **You can publish it elsewhere.** We do not claim exclusivity. You are free to post your work on your own site, in a journal, or anywhere else.
- **Your work is yours to submit.** By sending it to us, you are confirming you have the right to share it - it is your original work, or you have permission from whoever owns it.

If you ever want your content removed, contact us at [appliedam101@gmail.com](mailto:appliedam101@gmail.com) and we will handle it promptly.

## AI Usage Declaration

Some articles published on AppliedAM include a voluntary AI usage declaration. This declaration refers strictly to how the article was written and presented - not to the underlying research, experiments, or work described within it.

We use the **HMC (Human-Machine Collaboration)** classification framework developed by the Dubai Future Foundation. Five levels:

- **All Human** - no AI involvement in writing
- **Leader** - human led, AI assisted with checks, corrections, or enhancement
- **Collaborator** - human and AI worked together equally
- **Oversight** - AI led, human reviewed and corrected
- **All Machine** - entirely AI generated

Where disclosed, the article also shows which functions involved AI: Ideation, Literature Review, Data Collection, Data Analysis, Data Interpretation, Writing, Translation, Visuals, Design, or Instrument.

Declaration is voluntary. Where not shown, no assumption should be made either way.

Full framework: [dubaifuture.ae/hmc](https://dubaifuture.ae/hmc/)
